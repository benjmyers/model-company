Model Company

An uncommon look at the common Civil War soldier.

Charles Renard, far left, of Mess 2 poses with a friend from the 53rd Pennsylvania Infantry. Captain George A. Brooks, center, was killed in action at Antietam on Sept. 16th, 1862. Private Levi Ney, right, killed in action at Peach Tree Creek on July 21st, 1864. SOURCE: Private collection.
From top to bottom: Charles Renard, far left, of Mess 2 poses with a friend from the 53rd Pennsylvania Infantry. Captain George A. Brooks was killed in action at Antietam on Sept. 16th, 1862. Private Levi Ney was killed in action at Peach Tree Creek on July 21st, 1864. SOURCE: Private collection.
Mess information was collected from six lists of missing equipment, created after the Third Battle of Winchester on May 25th, 1862. SOURCE: Private collection.
Brothers Amos Wenrich, left, age 19, and John Wenrich, above, age 23, served in Mess 3. Amos drowned Jan. 31st, 1862 while guarding Dam. No. 6 outside of Hancock, MD. John was killed in action at Gettysburg, July 3rd, 1863. SOURCE: Private collection.
Brothers Amos Wenrich, age 19, and John Wenrich,age 23, in order from top to bottom, served in Mess 3. Amos drowned Jan. 31st, 1862 while guarding Dam. No. 6 outside of Hancock, MD. John was killed in action at Gettysburg, July 3rd, 1863. SOURCE: Private collection.
Brothers David and Henry Frantz (left and above respectively) served in Mess 3 for all four years of the war and returned to their homes in West Hanover Township outside of Harrisburg, PA. SOURCE: Private collection.
